The Philosophy of Balance

Yin: The Receptive Energy

Yin represents the dark, receptive, feminine, cooling, and inward aspects of existence. In the body, this corresponds to rest, fluids, nourishment, and stillness. When Yin is deficient, symptoms often include heat, anxiety, insomnia, and burnout.

  • Qualities: Calm, Rest, Water, Night, Cold.
  • Acupuncture Focus: Building fluids, cooling inflammation, grounding the spirit.

Yang: The Active Energy

Yang represents the light, active, masculine, warming, and outward aspects of existence. In the body, this corresponds to movement, metabolism, warmth, and function. When Yang is deficient, symptoms often include fatigue, cold limbs, poor digestion, and lethargy.

  • Qualities: Action, Movement, Fire, Day, Warmth.
  • Acupuncture Focus: Moving Qi/Blood, generating warmth, stimulating function.

"Health is not the absence of opposites, but their balanced, dynamic interchange."
